# Design System Blueprint

*This is the target. When the assistant builds your design system, this is the set of pages it produces. You don't fill this in by hand. The Design Interview does. It's here so you (and the assistant) know what "complete" looks like.*

*The structure mirrors how mature, real-world design systems are organized: from the soul outward to the applied output. Most pages can be drafted from your interview answers and existing materials; a few are optional until you need them.*

---

## Part 1 — Strategic Context (the soul)

*Why you exist and how things should feel. This is the part only a human can supply. It comes from the interview. Everything else flows from here.*

| Page | What it is | Where it comes from |
|---|---|---|
| **Brand Manifesto** | A short declaration: what you stand for, your core belief, your defining tenets, who you serve. Written in your voice. | Interview |
| **Design System Overview** | A one-page map of the whole system and how to use it. | Generated |
| **Taste & Composition** | The operating rules of good taste: the adjectives that describe your aesthetic, what to do and never do, how busy or calm things should feel. *This is your quality compass.* | Interview + your materials |
| **Approved Reference Images** | A small set of images that capture the right feeling: your "yes, like this." | You provide / curated |
| **Creative Brief** | How imagery, patterns, and overall direction should work. | Interview + your materials |

## Part 2 — Foundations (the building blocks)

*The concrete, reusable decisions. The assistant proposes these from your existing materials (your website, decks, logo) and you approve them.*

| Page | What it is |
|---|---|
| **Color System** | Your palette: primary, accents, neutrals, and the rules for when to use each. |
| **Typography** | Your fonts, sizes, and weights, for headlines, body, captions. |
| **Spacing & Layout** | The rhythm: margins, gaps, the grid everything sits on. |
| **Interactions** | How things behave when clicked or hovered (for anything interactive). |
| **Corners, Shadows & Depth** | Rounded vs. sharp, flat vs. layered: the texture of your look. |
| **Tokens** | The master list of every value above, named, so everything stays consistent. |
| **Composition Patterns** | Reusable page recipes (e.g., how a title section is laid out). |

## Part 3 — Components (the reusable pieces)

*Only built as you need them. For content work (decks, documents, one-pagers), the starred ones matter most.*

Buttons · Forms & Inputs · Cards · Tables · Tabs · Modals · Alerts · Badges · Avatars · Search bars · Empty states · Headers · Sidebars · Pagination · Charts & KPIs · **★ Logos & Marks** · **★ Slide Templates** · Iconography

## Part 4 — Applied / Designed Assets (the payoff)

*Real, finished templates you'll actually use: the reason the whole system exists.*

- **★ Letterhead & memo template**
- **★ Slide / presentation template**
- **★ One-pager / white-paper template**
- Landing page or hero section (if relevant to your work)
- Any recurring document you produce often

---

## How to read this as a non-technical user

Don't be intimidated by the length. In practice:

- **Parts 1 and 2 are the core:** your soul and your foundations. These get built first, from the interview.
- **Part 3** gets built piece by piece, only when a project needs it.
- **Part 4** is where it pays off: the templates you'll reuse constantly.

Your Coach will help you decide how far to go. Many people get enormous value from just Parts 1, 2, and a couple of Part 4 templates.
