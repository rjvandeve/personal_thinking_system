## Coach prompts

**Set up my thinking system**
> Be my coach and set up my thinking system. Start from the beginning, explain each step in plain language, ask me what you need, and do the building yourself. Check in with me before moving on.

**Resume my setup**
> Resume my setup where we left off. Remind me what's already in place, what's next, and pick up from there.

**Register the capabilities you just enabled**
> Update my capability ledger with whatever you just set up — what it does and how I use it. Keep it short and current so I always know what my system can do.

## Phase 1 — Memory

**Feed in a document**
> Please add this to memory. Here is [what it is] about [topic]. Read it, summarize the key points, file it under [work / learning / personal], and connect it to anything related. Tell me what you filed and linked.

**Brief me**
> Brief me on [project / topic] using everything in memory. Key points only, and tell me what you're drawing from.

**What do we know?**
> What do we know about [topic / person / question]? Pull only from memory, cite the pages you used, and flag anything missing.

**Stress-test me**
> I'm leaning toward [decision]. Based on what's in memory, push back. What might I be missing or getting wrong?

**Is that from memory?**
> Is that from memory, or are you guessing? Show me your sources.

**Save a good answer**
> That's worth keeping. Save it as a summary page in memory and link it to related pages.

**Tidy up (shows you the list first)**
> Tidy the memory. Give me a short, prioritized list: orphan pages, pages ready for takeaways, broken links, missing catalog entries, and outdated facts. Don't change anything yet — just show me.

## Phase 2 — Voice & Design

**Start the Style Interview**
> Let's build my design system. Interview me first — act as a brand strategist and design director. Work through the brand question bank as a real conversation, one or two questions at a time. Plain language, give me options to react to. When done, play back my manifesto, aesthetic adjectives, audience, and "never" list. Don't build yet.

**Feed it your materials**
> Study my existing materials — [decks / PDFs / website / logo]. Pull out my real colors, fonts, tone, and layouts. Tell me what's consistent, what's inconsistent, and what to keep vs. fix.

**Write the design system**
> Synthesize the interview and my materials into a written design system following the blueprint — soul, then foundations. Write the manifesto in my voice. Present section by section. Save a Voice & Style Card to memory.

**Build it in Figma (with coach)**
> Build this into Figma following the blueprint — Manifesto, Taste & Composition, then Foundations. Use the exact colors and fonts we agreed. One page at a time, screenshot each, refine before moving on.

**Run the Content Interview (sets your writing standards → house-style skill)**
> Let's set my house writing standards and build my house-style skill. Use the Content Production Playbook as the framework and interview me with the content question bank — quick multiple-choice. Cover locale, register, which rules to enforce, my personal banned/favored words, sourcing, and confidentiality. Pull my voice from my design system. Summarize before building.

**Start a long-form piece**
> Let's produce a [white paper / long-form post] following the Content Production Playbook. First help me nail the single sharp point in one or two sentences. Don't draft yet — we'll research, study my voice, lock the brief, and outline for sign-off first.

**Draft on-brand content** *(house-style applies your standards automatically)*
> Using my Voice & Style Card and memory about [topic], draft a [memo / post / email / one-pager] for [audience]. In my voice. [length / the one point / CTA].

**Make it sound like me**
> This reads like generic AI. Rewrite it to sound like me per my Voice & Style Card — my phrasing, my rhythm, cut anything I'd never say. Less hedging, more conviction.

**Taste pass** *(also triggers automatically once the skill is installed)*
> Run a Jobs–Ive taste pass before I send this. First pass: attack the concept — the one thing it must do, what's confused, what to cut, the braver version. Second pass: refine the craft — what doesn't earn its place, where it's generic, the small fixes. Give me both, then a revised version. Hold to my Voice & Style Card and design system.

**Diagnose a bad feeling**
> This feels [cramped / empty / noisy / flat / confusing / dated / accidental]. Diagnose why and fix it.

## Phase 3 — Building

**Describe what you want**
> I want to build a small tool for myself. The problem: [annoyance]. What I wish I could do: [outcome]. Don't build anything yet — ask me questions until you understand it well enough to plan.

**Get a plan first**
> Make a plan before building anything. In plain language: what we'll build, the main pieces, the order, and what could go wrong. Don't build yet — I'll review it with my coach.

**Break it into small pieces**
> Break this plan into the smallest sensible pieces — each small, finishable, and provable. For each, tell me what it does and how we'll know it works. List them in order.

**Build one piece**
> Let's build only the first piece: [name it]. Just that one. When it's done, stop and show me.

**Prove it works**
> Prove this piece works. Show me how you checked and let me see the result.

**Capture an idea (don't chase it)**
> Capture this as a task for later — don't build it now: [idea]. Then let's get back to the piece we were on.

**Slow it down**
> Stop — one step at a time. Show me before you change anything.

**Get back to safety**
> Something doesn't seem right. Stop making changes. Tell me, in plain language: the current state, the last thing that worked, and our options to get back to a good state.

---

## The four habits (the thread through all of it)

1. **Knowledge should compound** — give the assistant a memory; don't re-explain your world.
2. **Prove it worked** — never accept "done"; ask to see the result and how it was checked.
3. **One thing at a time** — small, single, finished steps beat big tangled ones.
4. **Capture, don't chase** — write new ideas down instead of dropping everything to do them.
