# Helper — Keeping Your Memory Tidy

*Every so often, ask the assistant to clean up after itself. A tidy memory stays trustworthy; a messy one quietly rots.*

---

## The idea in one sentence

Like any filing system, your memory benefits from an occasional once-over — finding gaps, fixing broken links, and flagging facts that have gone stale. The assistant does the work; you just approve it.

## When to do it

- After feeding in a burst of material (say, 5+ documents).
- Roughly once a month if you use it steadily.
- Any time an answer feels off or out of date.

There's no harm in doing it more often. It's a read-and-report, so it never breaks anything without your say-so.

## How to do it

> Please tidy the memory. Run a cleanup check and give me a short, prioritized list of what you found: orphan pages nothing links to, pages that are ready for takeaways, broken links, anything missing from the catalog, and any facts that newer material has made outdated. **Don't change anything yet — just show me the list.**

## What it will report

A short list like:

- **Outdated:** The "Pricing" page says $40; the doc you fed last week says $45.
- **Gap:** You mention "the partnership" across three pages but there's no page for it.
- **Loose end:** The "Q3 Review" page isn't linked from anything.

## Then you decide

Review the list and tell it what to fix:

> Go ahead and fix the outdated pricing and create the partnership page. Leave the rest for now.

It makes only the changes you approved and notes them in the history.

## Why this matters more than it seems

Memory you can't trust is worse than no memory — it gives confident wrong answers. A monthly tidy keeps the assistant honest: stale facts get caught, gaps get filled, and the knowledge base stays something you can rely on for real decisions.

## The one rule

**It always shows you the list before changing anything.** If it ever proposes changes you didn't ask to see first, tell it to stop and report first. You're always in control of your own record.
