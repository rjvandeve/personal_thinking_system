# Helper — Getting Briefed & Asking Questions

*How to ask the assistant anything and get an answer grounded in your own accumulated knowledge — with sources — instead of a generic guess.*

---

## The idea in one sentence

Because the assistant has a memory now, it can answer from *your* world first: your documents, your projects, your past decisions — and tell you where each part of the answer came from.

## The three most useful asks

**1. Brief me.**
> Brief me on **[project / topic]** using everything in memory. Keep it to the key points, and tell me what you're drawing from.

**2. What do we know about…?**
> What do we know about **[topic / person / question]**? Pull only from memory, cite the pages you used, and flag anything we're missing.

**3. Stress-test my thinking.**
> I'm leaning toward **[your decision or plan]**. Based on what's in memory, push back on this. What does our own material suggest I might be missing or getting wrong?

## Why "cite the pages" matters

Always ask it to tell you what it's drawing from. Two reasons:

- You can trust an answer you can trace. If it cites the March board deck, you know it's grounded.
- If it *can't* cite anything, that's your signal the memory has a gap — feed in more, or treat the answer as a general guess rather than fact.

## Turning a good answer into permanent knowledge

Sometimes the assistant produces an answer so useful you'll want it again later. Just say:

> That's worth keeping. Save it as a summary page in memory and link it to the related pages.

Now it's part of the compounding knowledge base, not a one-off.

## A habit worth building

Before any meeting, decision, or piece of writing, start with: *"Brief me on [the thing] from memory."* Thirty seconds of being brought up to speed by something that remembers everything you've told it. This is the single most valuable habit in the whole kit.

## Watch out for

- **An answer with no sources** = either a gap in memory or the assistant reaching beyond what it knows. Ask: *"Is that from memory or are you guessing?"*
- **An overconfident answer.** Ask it to flag what's uncertain. A good brief tells you what it *doesn't* know too.
